There is much to be learned about cognition and memory in otter species. Utilizing a recent collaborative research model in which multiple researchers and facilities agree to investigate and conduct one research project in an effort to increase sample sizes and generalizable results, we plan to create a large-scale collaboration to investigate cognitive projects with otters (all species) utilizing a network of scientists representing multiple zoos/aquariums across the country (ManyOtters). Past research has shown mixed evidence for long-term memory in otters (Ladds et al., 2017; Saliveros et al., 2020; Wegman & DeLong, 2023). To further test long-term memory in otters, three facilities have been selected to pilot our initial project in which otter long-term memory will be assessed using novel puzzle feeders: SeaWorld of Texas (Manitzas Hill), Beardsley Zoo (Yeater), Seneca Park Zoo (DeLong). At these facilities, we would like to pilot 5-15 novel puzzle feeders in Oct-Nov 2023 to the two species of otters currently housed in these facilities (North American river otters and Asian small clawed otters). Approximately 1-3 months later, pending schedules, the otters would be given the same puzzle feeders to solve. All puzzle feeder sessions will be videotaped for scoring otter responses to the puzzles. Pending approval of the proposed methodology, the pilot study is intended to evaluate the research protocol and to establish a standardized methodology that can be shared with other facilities and researchers in preparation for the ManyOtters 1 Project. Ultimately, we expect that our findings will have applications for animal welfare in zoos and can inform conservation efforts for otters.

Project Decisions

Proposals for possible research projects may be submitted to the leadership team for consideration from all members. The leadership team will then identify top priorities of proposed research and will request ALL members to vote on the list of selected projects.
